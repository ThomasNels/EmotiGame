EmotiGame

The Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Nevada, Reno
CS 426 Senior Project
Spring 2025

Methodology:

Our study investigates the potential relationship between biometric readings and skill level in competitive video gaming, specifically in League of Legends. The goal is to identify measurable patterns, such as heart rate, mouse movement, and keyboard usage, that may correlate with player skill during gameplay.

Participants

When recruiting participants for our study, we looked for people over the age of 18 with no restrictions on race, gender, or ethnicity. We recruited participants by putting up a flyer around campus and we were looking for participants with a wide range of skills and experience in League of Legends.

We wanted at least six unskilled and six skilled participants. We classified our unskilled players as people who were either unranked in the game or had a ranking of Silver or lower, also known as the bottom 52 percentile of players. Our skilled participants were classified as having a rank Emerald or higher, with the highest rank being Master. Participants in these ranks were among the top 28 percentile of players.

Each of our participants had to play against our control player, who had a ranking of master, and is told they will receive $10 if they win. This control was recruited directly from the University of Nevada, Reno's E-Sports club.

Procedure

For each session, our control player and participants are placed in separate rooms. After reading the consent form, an EmotiBit heart rate sensor is placed on their upper arm. They are then told they can change the settings of the game however they would like. The EmotiBit begins recording while they do this to establish a baseline heart rate.

After 5-10 minutes, a 1v1 League of Legends match begins between the two and is considered over when one player dies, when 50 enemy minions are killed, or when the enemy tower is destroyed. Once the match is over, the participant is asked to complete two post-game surveys. One asks questions about the previous game play experience and the other is a random combination of the Player Experience of Needs Satisfaction (PENS) [9] and the Igroup Presence Questionnaire (IPQ) [10] surveys. Our control player took the gameplay survey after every session and PENS and IPQ survey once after all the study trials were complete. These surveys were hosted on our website that was created using Flask, Python, and PostgreSQL.

Once the surveys have been completed, the EmotiBit is taken off and the participant receives $10 regardless of sucess. In addition, the control player receives $10 for every match played. Both are asked to keep it secret to preserve the integrity of the other trials.

Data Collection and Analysis

During each trial we record the heart rate of the control player and the participant. The recording starts 5-10 minutes before each match and ends after the surveys have been filled out. Additionally, mouse and keyboard tracking software collects data on the mouse and keyboard movements of both players during the match. All data is anonymized and stored securely with access restricted to the core research team and principal investigator. Visit our results page for more infomation on the analysis.
