Results of our study

To help analyze biometric differences in skill level, we employed various machine learning models to classify participants based on heart rate and movement during specific gameplay events. We trained Long Short-term Memory (LSTM), Random Forest, and Support Vector Machine (SVM) classifiers on features that included participant heart rate, base heart rate, actions per minute, and key event labels. By comparing model performance, we identified a correlation between biometric indicators and player skill level. The LSTM model was particularly useful for capturing temporal patterns in the features, offering insight into how players respond to different in-game situations. Overall, the results suggest that biometric data can serve as an indicator of skill level in video game players and provide a foundation for future research in this area.

Figure 2 compares the mean actions per minute (AMP) among three groups: control, unskilled, and skilled. The skilled participants have high AMP and the unskilled have the lowest. This contrast between the two can indicate that there is a performance gap tied to their expertise.

Figure 3: Mean heart rate (HR) during an event by group.

This graph, figure 3, compares mean heart rate across control, skilled, and unskilled groups during important events. The skilled participants have the highest heart rate, then the control player. The lower heart rate of the unskilled participants could reflect less investment in the game.

Table 1: Accuracy of the machine learning models used.

Table 1 shows the performance of the machine learning models we used. Accuracy is the overall correctness of the model. Precision is the proportion of correct classifications. Higher precision means fewer false positives. Recall is the ability to correctly identify all instances of a given skill level. The higher the recall is, the fewer detections were missed. F1 - Score is the harmonic mean of precision and recall and provides a balanced metric when both are implemented. As seen in the table above, All of our models performed very well. They had a very good accuracy and provided a lot of insight into our results.

Table 2: Statistical significance between skill level and biometric readings for different types of events.

Table 2 shows the statistical significance for the relationship between skill level and biometric responses, like heart rate or AMP, across in-game events of varying importance. A low p-value, typically less than 0.05, indicates that the observed differences are statistically significant.

After reading the values within table 2, we see that there is some statistical significance between AMP with high importance events, as well as statistical significance between heart rate with low and high importance events. While we don't fully understand how yet, table 2 shows that there is a correlation of skill level with AMP and heart rate.
